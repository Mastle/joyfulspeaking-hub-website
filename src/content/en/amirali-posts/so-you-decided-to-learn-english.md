## Discipline
Discipline is the foundation of learning any foreign language.

It is the first item on the list that every language learner should focus on. It is the bedrock that makes all language learning efforts meaningful. Therefore, instilling discipline in yourself is crucial when starting to learn English. This minimizes the risk of experiencing problems such as burnout, information overload, or failure to acquire the mindset of a native speaker. Needless to say, achieving such a mindset is necessary to make progress as a learner.

On the other hand, having self-discipline in your learning can enhance your performance in every possible way. Learning a language is a mental process, and therefore, it's essential to ensure that your mind is a clean and organized space for this process to occur. Consistency in learning is the key to mental sharpness, and this consistency is only achievable through discipline.

It's easy to determine if you have the right level of self-discipline to start learning.

Start by dedicating a small amount of your free time every week for a month to an English-related activity. Even if you have only 30 minutes to spare for the whole week, merely interacting with English content for 30 minutes every week for a month is a significant step towards improving your English. You will naturally want to increase this number in the next months after you see the progress that you have been able to make with your previous schedule

You can choose any leisure activity such as watching your favorite movie, reading the lyrics of your favorite songs, or learning three new words to begin learning English.

Keep in mind that you don't have to focus on educational activities in the first month of your learning journey and can opt for entertainment purposes only. In fact, I recommend that most beginners opt for entertainment activities to make the process more enjoyable in the beginning The most important thing is to monitor how well you stick to your plan. If you can adhere to the plan that you created for yourself, it means you have accomplished the most difficult aspect of preparing to learn English!

Even if you have inconsistently followed this schedule, it still means that you are ready to learn.

As long as you did not completely give up, even if you had to postpone a few days, you have successfully taken the first step.

## Love of Learning
The second ingredient is love of learning. It is vital to engage with English learning materials that are suitable for you. This means that the materials should be interesting and relatable to you. In other words, the materials you select should be compatible with your personality and individuality.

You might be a person who enjoys technology and prefers a direct approach to learning, so starting with a language-learning app would be the right choice for you. On the other hand, if you have a strong interest in 80s and 90s mafia movies, you can incorporate this into your study strategy to make it more engaging and effective. No matter what your preference is, there's always a way to make it work for you.

What is important is to ensure that the materials you use to learn English are interesting to you and motivate you to keep learning. Additionally, the difficulty level of the content is also a factor that has to be considered.

## Proper outlet
The final step is to ensure that you have a way to express yourself regularly. For the first two months or so, this step is optional. You can ignore it if you are not used to the previous steps yet.

By this point, you will have gained a lot of knowledge through the combination of discipline and suitable learning materials. However, just acquiring new vocabulary and grammar rules in English won't enable you to use the language fluently. The final piece of the puzzle is to put your knowledge into practice, which will help you to speak and write English confidently.

Finding a way or reason to use the new knowledge you've gained is vital, even if it's something as small as using one new word in a sentence once a week. Trying to express yourself using the new things you've learned, even if you're not always successful, can greatly improve your English skills.

If you have someone with the same or higher level of English proficiency as you, they can be a great partner for you to try this with.

The previous step advised against doing too much in the beginning and recommended incremental handling. The same principle applies here.

If you require sentence review but lack someone to check them, feel free to send them to us via our various social media platforms. We'll gladly assist you at no cost.

We all started as beginners who struggled with basic language skills, the only difference is we persevered and kept practicing until we improved. This same dedication is what has led language teachers and fluent speakers of English to their success.

If you manage to get things right on your first try, congratulations! However, even if you make mistakes in consecutive attempts, you should view it as an opportunity to learn and identify areas for improvement.
